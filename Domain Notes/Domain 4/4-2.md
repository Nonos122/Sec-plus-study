# 4.2 Notes

## Acquisition/Procurement Process

The stage where new hardware, software, or services are formally evaluated and obtained. Security should be considered before purchase — vetting vendors, reviewing security track records, checking for supply chain risk, and ensuring contracts include appropriate security and support terms — rather than being addressed only after the asset is already in use.

## Assignment/Accounting

Tracking who is responsible for an asset once it's acquired, and how it's categorized.

- Ownership — assigning a specific person or team as accountable for an asset, including its security and proper use.
- Classification — labeling an asset based on its sensitivity or importance (e.g., critical, restricted), so appropriate handling and protection requirements can be applied.

## Monitoring/Asset Tracking

Ongoing visibility into what assets exist and where they are.

- Inventory — a maintained, accurate record of all hardware and software assets the organization owns or uses.
- Enumeration — actively scanning or querying the environment to discover and list assets, helping catch unauthorized or unknown ("shadow IT") devices that aren't in the official inventory.

## Disposal/Decommissioning

The formal process of retiring an asset securely once it's no longer needed, ensuring it doesn't become a lingering security risk.

- Sanitization — removing all recoverable data from a device before it's reused, resold, or disposed of (e.g., wiping a drive).
- Destruction — physically destroying storage media (shredding, degaussing, incineration) when sanitization isn't sufficient or the media won't be reused.
- Certification — obtaining documented proof (often from a third party) that data destruction or sanitization was performed correctly, supporting compliance and audit requirements.
- Data retention — following defined policies for how long data must be kept before it can be securely destroyed, based on legal, regulatory, or business requirements.

5. What's the difference between sanitization and destruction, and when would you use one over the other?
6. Why is certification of data destruction important, even after the data has actually been destroyed?
7. What's the difference between a data retention policy and a data sanitization policy?
8. If a company disposes of an old server without properly sanitizing its drives, what security risks could result?
9. Why does asset classification need to happen early in an asset's lifecycle rather than just before disposal?
