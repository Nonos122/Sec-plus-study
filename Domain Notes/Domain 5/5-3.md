# 5.3 Notes


## Vendor Assessment

1. Penetration testing — having a vendor undergo (or provide results from) simulated attacks to validate their security controls actually work.
2. Right-to-audit clause — a contract term giving your organization the right to inspect or audit a vendor's security practices directly.
3. Evidence of internal audits — reviewing a vendor's own internal audit results to confirm they're actively checking their own compliance and controls.
4. Independent assessments — third-party evaluations (e.g., SOC 2 reports) of a vendor's security posture, offering an unbiased view beyond the vendor's self-reporting.
5. Supply chain analysis — evaluating the vendor's own suppliers and dependencies, since a weakness further up their supply chain can still expose your organization.

## Vendor Selection

1. Due diligence — thoroughly researching and evaluating a vendor's security, financial stability, and reputation before entering into a relationship with them.
2. Conflict of interest — identifying any situation where a vendor's other relationships or incentives could compromise their objectivity or the integrity of the services they provide.

## Agreement Types

1. SLA (Service Level Agreement) — defines measurable performance expectations (uptime, response time) a vendor must meet.
2. MOA (Memorandum of Agreement) — a formal, documented agreement between parties outlining a mutual understanding, more binding than an MOU but less detailed than a full contract.
3. MOU (Memorandum of Understanding) — a non-binding statement of mutual intent between parties, less formal than a contract.
4. MSA (Master Service Agreement) — a foundational contract establishing the overall terms that will govern future work, allowing individual projects to be added via smaller agreements (like an SOW) without renegotiating everything each time.
5. SOW (Statement of Work) — defines the specific scope, deliverables, timeline, and cost of a particular project or engagement, often under an existing MSA.
6. NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement) — legally protects confidential information shared between parties from being disclosed to others.
7. BPA (Business Partnership Agreement) — defines the terms of a business partnership, including ownership stakes, responsibilities, and profit/loss sharing where applicable.

## Vendor Monitoring

Ongoing review of a vendor's security posture and performance throughout the relationship, not just during initial selection — since a vendor's risk profile can change over time due to new vulnerabilities, incidents, or changes in their own environment.

## Questionnaires

Standardized sets of security questions sent to a vendor (or prospective vendor) to assess their controls, policies, and practices, providing a documented, comparable baseline across multiple vendors.

## Rules of Engagement

A documented agreement, established before a security assessment or penetration test begins, defining the scope, boundaries, allowed techniques, timing, and points of contact — ensuring the assessment stays authorized and controlled and doesn't cause unintended disruption.
